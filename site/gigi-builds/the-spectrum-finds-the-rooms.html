The Spectrum Finds the Rooms

Seventy-two nodes, three communities — and nobody laid this graph out. The 3D coordinates you are looking at are the graph's own Laplacian eigenvectors, recomputed live every time you touch the slider. When the bridges are thick the spectrum sees one room and the constellation huddles; cut them and λ₁ collapses toward zero while the three communities physically tear apart — the layout isn't illustrating the math, the layout is the math.

layout = eigenvectors 1, 2, 3 of the graph Laplacian · recomputed live (Jacobi, 72×72)
drag to orbit · wheel to zoom
community A community B community C bridge edge

This is what the engine's SPECTRAL verb is measuring when it reports the gap. λ₁ near zero means "this bundle's relation graph is about to disconnect" — communities that barely talk — and the same eigenvectors that detect it are good coordinates for seeing it, which is the whole idea of spectral embedding. Watch the moment you drag to one bridge per pair: the rooms are already visibly separate while λ₁ is still nonzero. The spectrum hears the wall before the wall is finished.
